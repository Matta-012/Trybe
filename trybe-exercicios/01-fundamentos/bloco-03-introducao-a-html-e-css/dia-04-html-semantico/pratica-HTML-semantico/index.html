Facts about the Stomatopoda

General Information

Odontodactylus scyllarus

Kingdom: Animalia
Phylum: Arthropoda
Subphylum: Crustacea
Class: Malacostraca
Subclass: Hoplocarida
Order: Stomatopoda

Color Vision Supremacy

The mantis shrimp lives in warm, shallow water, and typically grows to be between 6-12 inches (15-30cm) in length. This marvelous creature has not two, not three, not five, but SIXTEEN color-receptive cones and have the most sophisticated vision of ANY PREDATOR ON EARTH.

The rainbow we see stems from just THREE colors, so try to imagine a mantis rainbow created from SIXTEEN colors. Where we see a rainbow, the mantis shrimp sees a TERMONUCLEAR BOMB of light and beauty.

The Undersea Nightmare

Despite being very beautiful, the mantis shrimp is EXTREMELY VIOLENT. It has two raptorial appendages on the front of it's body. These acclerate with the same velocity as a gunshot from a .22 caliber rifle, and in less than three-thousandsths of a second can strike prey with 1,500 Newtons of force!

To put this in perspective, if human beings could accelerate our arms at 1/10th that speed, we'd be able to throw a baseball into orbit!

Their murder sticks limbs move so quickly the water around them BOILS in a process know as supercavitation. When these cavitation bubbles collapse it produces an undersea shockwave that kill prey even if the mantis shrimp misses it's target. The force of these collapsing bubbles also produces temperatures that macthes the THE SURFACE OF THE SUN and emits tiny bursts of light.

Using these murder sticks limbs, DESMEMBERMENT is primarily how the mantis shrimp kills it's prey. It bashes other animals into pieces.
Aquariums don't tipically house mantis shrimps because they tend to slaughter every other creature they share a tank with and also because they
CAN BREAK AQUARIUM GLASS.
